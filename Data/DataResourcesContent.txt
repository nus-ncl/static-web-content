          Explore the DFIR Dataset from Locked Shields 2025 — The World’s Biggest Military-Level Cyber Exercise
        </u>
      </h2>
      <p>
This Digital Forensics dataset was developed as part of DFIR data resource for the largest and most advanced International Cyber Defense exercise -- <a href="https://www.ccdcoe.org/news/2025/nations-unite-under-pressure-as-locked-shields-2025-kicks-off-in-tallinn">Locked Shields 2025</a> which is organized by the <a href="https://ccdcoe.org/"> NATO Cooperative Cyber Defence Centre of Excellence (CCDCOE)</a>.  It captures realistic, full-area forensic data from both IT and OT environments, making it a valuable resource for cybersecurity training, incident reconstruction, CTF development, and academic research.
      </p>
    </div>
<p>&nbsp;</p>

<p style="margin-left: 40px; margin-right: 40px;"> Collected over a continuous 9-hour period, the dataset reflects a successful multi-stage cyberattack involving various attack scenarios across interconnected IT and OT systems. From initial compromise to lateral movement and impact on operational processes, the data provides a rare opportunity to study how complex attacks evolve in real time.This dataset is ideal for those looking to explore attack detection, response workflows, or forensic analysis in environments that combine traditional networks with industrial infrastructure. Whether you are designing training programs, building labs, or testing tools, this data offers a rich, realistic foundation.
